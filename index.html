Is it safe to email?

It isn't very frequent that we email sensitive information, but if you're unsure, check this reference site!

SSL Certificates

Most of the confusion here comes with not knowing the different pieces that make up an SSL certificate. Here are the pieces and the recommended action:

  • Private key - Never email this. See the alternate methods below.
  • Public key aka CSR - This is totally safe to email.
  • SSL Certificate(s) - Usually when you purchase an SSL cert, you'll get several certificates. They're all safe to email. In fact, they're usually emailed to you from the vendor.

Credit Card Information

No. Never.

Alternative Methods

Email is like a postcard - there's no protection for preying eyes. Here are some alternative methods to send sensitive information:

  • Services like Dropbox, Google Drive, etc. - Upload the sensitive information and then share the folder with the Dropbox account of the recipient. Important: do *not* simply copy and share the public link to the sensitive information.
  • Diversify methods - If you have multiple pieces of information that need to be sent, don't send them all in the same method. Send one via a text message, one via Dropbox, and one via a phone call. Although not 100% safe, you'll lower the chance of an attacker collecting all of the pieces and putting them together.
